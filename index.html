Automated Impedance Tube

One of my senior design projects at the University of Miami. It was published and presented at the Audio Engineering Society's 138th conference in Warsaw, Poland.

One of the fundamental tools of acoustics is an impedance tube. It measures the way a substance interacts with different frequencies. Current tubes are expensive, cumbersum, and hard to use so we developed an easy to use, lightweight solution for under $150. Shout out to my buds, Connor McCullough and Diego Ugaz, for the great work!

  • Date: February 2015
  • Client: University of Miami
  • Category: Acoustics Engineering

Impulse Response Measurments

Popping ballons in a hallway to quantify its reverb decay times.

This project goal was to follow ISO-3382, which asked us to record 12 impulse responses in a room, in order to calculate the rooms reverb coefficients. The measurements took place in a two-story stairwell in the McArthur Engineering Annex and the impulse response used was a balloon popping. There were three microphone positions and four balloon positions. The microphone was placed on the top of the stairwell, in the middle of the stairwell, and on the bottom of the stairwell. The microphone was on a stand and the diaphragm was raised to 6 feet off the ground. The balloons were popped on the first step, on the last step before the middle, on the first step after the middle, and the last step. The diagrams below document the recording positions and procedures that took place. The instruments used were an Apogee Duet 2 recording interface (24- bit @ 44.1kHz Sampling Frequency), an Orange Spark Microphone (Cardioid Condenser Diaphragm with Mogami cabling), and Audacity (Open-Source Digital Audio Workstation). The audio was recorded at the specified settings but was exported as 16-bit linear PCM WAV files for MATLAB.

  • Date: December 2014
  • Client: Univrsity of Miami
  • Category: Acoustics MATLAB
